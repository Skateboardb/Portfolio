For small businesses, this means that it would probably be a good idea to look into making the transition into utilizing the business functions that are built into social networks.

Facebook is considered in many ways to be be the most powerful advertising platform when compared with other social media and with more traditional advertising. This article outlines twelve reasons that your business could probably benefit from effective use of Facebook's Business Manager tools.

1. Userbase

As of the 4th Quarter of 2017, there were over 2 billion active users per month; that is to say, with the widest reach of any other social media platform, Facebook isn't expected to go anywhere anytime soon.

2. Activity

According to Facebook's own most recent report on the subject, there are more than 1.4 billion people logging on using various devices every day. Not only are there a lot of people using it, but the vast majority of those who do are doing so very consistently.

3. Data

Facebook has revealed that their data centers process over 500 terabytes of data every single day. This includes the wealth of information that is collected so that ads are delivered to the right people.

4. Targeting

All of that public data collected by Facebook can be put to good use by targeting core audiences for advertising. The mechanisms for doing just this are so robust that 95% of social media experts agree that Facebook is the most valuable tool for accomplishing a variety of tasks with their ads including brand awareness, lead generation, and conversion.

5. Local-Friendly

Although there are well over 50 million businesses with pages on facebook, only about 10% of them are actively using Business Manageer to promote their posts; this means that it doesn't reqire a huge ad budget to ensure that you're reaching your local audience.

6. Community Engagement

A large part of the purpose of using Facebook's business pages even without advertising is for the sake of having an online presence where your audience already is. It's good to have a website, but a page where members of the community are reacting to your posts and communicating with each other is just the sort of friendly atmosphere that helps expand your organic reach.

7. Reviews

Not long ago, sites like Yelp were the go-to source of information about public opinion regarding restaurants. These days, people who are already scrolling through their Facebook feeds are likely to look for what they might want to eat without leaving the app or site. Further, the reviews on Business Pages come across as more personable and authentic because users can get a glimpse of the people leaving the messages, for better or worse. Rections to ads are powerful tools for inviting people who are already interested in what you have to offer to like your page. This gives you a better chance of reaching them both through paid advertising and organically in the future.

8. Algorithm Changes

I've made a couple of statements about organic reach, so let's talk about that - unless a post goes viral (which is a real possibility if you post good quality content), organic reach is decreasing overall for business pages. Business reach has long been approaching a pay-to-play status, and the time has come for businesses to consider including paid reach on Facebook in any marketing strategy.

9. Trackable

One of the best things about Facebook ads is the ability to monitor the performance of your ads, so there's never any guesswork involved with determining the efficacy of a particular ad campaign. Whether you're running the ads yourself or working with a specialist, you should be able to see exactly how well each ad is doing and measure it against your standard for success.

10. Testable

It's valuable enough to have information on how well an ad is doing; what's better is having the ability to 'share' an ad budget between two versions of the same ad to help detect seemingly minor differences between advertisements which can actually make a huge impact on an ad's performance such as a change in wording in the copy or a different image in the creative.

11. Scalable

Once you're got a winning formula with an adset, it's easy to create an increase in response by simply increasing the ad budget. In other words, you can personally reach the people who work in a block around your location or put your content in front of everyone within a metropolitain area.

12. Lowest CPC

Perhaps the most exciting thing of all about Facebook advertising is that it is in a place of incredible effectiveness and very low barrier to entry. To put things in perspective, the last major platform to offer such inexpensive and effective advertising was Google with AdWords in the early 2000s. Anyone who wastes no time in moving into paid promotion on Facebook will be poised to benefit from a major marketing opportunity the likes of which will not be seen again for quite some time.
